Concussion Treatment in Yorkton, Saskatchewan

Headaches, brain fog, dizziness, trouble with screens — a concussion can throw off your whole week. Our concussion care helps you return to work, sport, and life at a pace your brain can handle.

Book an Appointment

Understanding concussion

A concussion affects how your brain is working for a while, and the symptoms can be frustratingly invisible to everyone but you. Rest has its place early on, but we now know that the right, gradual return to activity matters too. We help guide that balance.

Signs we can help with

Concussion symptoms we work with include:

  • Headaches and pressure in the head
  • Dizziness, balance trouble, or feeling 'off'
  • Brain fog, trouble concentrating, or fatigue
  • Sensitivity to light, screens, or noise
  • Neck pain that came with the injury

How we can help

We assess the different systems a concussion can affect — including the neck and balance — and build a gradual, guided plan toward your normal activities. We'll coordinate with other providers when your recovery needs it.

What to expect at your first visit

We'll take a careful history of your injury and symptoms, do a thorough assessment, and start a plan paced to you. You'll leave knowing what to do — and what not to overdo — between visits.
